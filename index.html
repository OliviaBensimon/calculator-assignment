Comparing the price of groceries at supermarkets in Manhattan

By Olivia Bensimon | April 13, 2021

Supermarkets, C-Town photo courtesy of CaptJayRuffins, Key Food and Gristedes photo by Olivia Bensimon, Fairway photo courtesy of Dougie WII

Last spring, as the pandemic swept through New York City, public health experts and government entities encouraged people to stay home to “stop the spread” and stay safe. As many people lost their main source of income, they turned to food pantries for support, and more people became eligible for SNAP benefits.

But in many cases, with only so much food to distribute at local food pantries and soup kitchens, New Yorkers could only pick up groceries there once a month and had to rely on local supermarkets as an alternative. The interactive calculator below compares the price of items that would typically be found in a food pantry package at various (supposedly “low cost”) grocery stores in Manhattan.
